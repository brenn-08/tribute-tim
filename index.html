Tim Berners-Lee

The man who changed the world

Tim Berners-Lee is the inventor of the World Wide Web, revolutionizing how information is shared and accessed globally, shaping the internet as we know it today.

Here's a timeline of Tim's life:

  • Jun 1955 - Born in London, to engineers of the world's first commercially built computer, Timothy John Berners-Lee was destined for greatness from an early age.
  • Sep 1969 - Went to Emanuel School in London from 1969 to 1973.
  • May 1973 - Graduated from Queen's College, Oxford where he recieved 1st class degree in physics.
  • Jan 1976 - Tim had the knowledge and experience with building computers and now it was time to start computer languages. He spent a few years developing them.
  • Jan 1978 - Tim worked at D.G. Nash Limited where he wrote typesetting software.
  • Jun 1980 - Started working at CERN. Berners-Lee worked as a freelance consultant for the physics research facility.
  • Mar 1989 - Writes first proposal for the World Wide Web. Originally described as a "large hyperlink database with typed links", his proposal wasn't very popular, but he boss encouraged him to test it out on a computer.
  • Oct 1990 - Started work on World Wide Web.
  • Aug 1991 - This day, the world wide web was launched on the internet making it available for the entire public world.
  • Jan 1993 - Mosaic Web Browser launched. The Mosaic Web Browser is the first application that popularized the internet.
  • Oct 1994 - World Wide Web Consortium established. Berners-Lee created a standards organization for the internet at MIT.
  • Jan 1997 - Last release and subsequent end of Mosaic Browser.
  • Nov 1999 - In 1999, Time Magazine included Tim Berners-Lee in their list of the 100 most influential minds of the twentieth century.
  • Jul 2004 - Knighted by the Order of the British Empire. Promoted from Officer of the OBE to Knight of the OBE. Now officially referred to as Sir Tim Berners-Lee.
  • Dec 2004 - He became a professor in the Computer Science Department at the University of Southampton, UK, in December 2004. There he worked on the Semantic Web.
  • Jun 2009 - Begins working for the UK government. Berners-Lee was asked by the UK government to help make online data more safe and accessible for the public
  • Nov 2009 - World Wide Web Foundation launched. Tim launched a charity called the World Wide Web Foundation in Uganda in 2009. This foundation goes towards the improvement and development of the world wide web. It's purpose is to "advance the Web to empower humanity by launching transformative programs that build local capacity to leverage the Web as a medium for positive change."
  • May 2012 - President of Open Data Institute.
  • Oct 2013 - Alliance for Affordable Internet launched. An initiative to bring affordable internet connection to all parts of the globe. Partners include: Google, Facebook, Intel, & Microsoft.
